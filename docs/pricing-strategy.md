# Pricing Strategy

## Positioning

Followup should sell one simple promise first:

- recover warm leads sitting in Gmail
- cut reply friction with fast AI drafts
- give solo operators a lightweight pipeline without a heavy CRM

## Starter Plans

### Solo

- `$19/month`
- built for freelancers and solo consultants
- includes 1 Gmail account, unlimited follow-up scans, AI draft generation, reminders, and weekly value reporting

### Studio

- `$49/month`
- built for very small agencies or partnership teams
- includes up to 3 inboxes, shared lead notes, reminder workflows, analytics, and onboarding help

## Founding Offer

Use a simple founder-friendly offer first:

- 14-day free trial
- discounted founding-user pricing during beta
- optional concierge onboarding for the first 10 customers

## Why This Pricing Works

- low enough for freelancers to try without a long approval cycle
- high enough to prove real business value if the product recovers even one lead
- simple enough to explain in one sentence during direct outreach

## What To Validate First

Before expanding plans, confirm:

1. users connect Gmail successfully
2. users see at least one useful flagged follow-up
3. users generate a draft and understand the value quickly
4. at least a few users say the product is worth paying for at the current price

## Next Monetization Step

The next implementation milestone is Stripe checkout plus a lightweight trial flow so the pricing page can convert into real paid subscriptions.
